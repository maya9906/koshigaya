KOSHIGAYA OHASHI

There is a large white bridge in Koshigaya City known as the "Koshigaya Ohashi." Below is an overview of the history of the Koshigaya Ohashi: The Koshigaya Ohashi is a bridge that spans the Edo River, which flows through Koshigaya City. It is recognized as a local landmark. The bridge's official name is the "Edogawa Dai-san Kyouyou" or "Edogawa Third Bridge." Construction of the Koshigaya Ohashi took place in 1960. At the time, Koshigaya City was undergoing urbanization, and with the increasing traffic volume, there was a need for new transportation infrastructure. As a result, the construction of the Koshigaya Ohashi was realized. The Koshigaya Ohashi is known for its white truss design, featuring a beautiful arch shape. This design harmonizes with the surrounding landscape and is well-loved by local residents and visitors alike. The construction of the Koshigaya Ohashi not only provided transportation benefits but also contributed to the development of the region. The completion of the bridge revitalized exchanges and interactions between Koshigaya City and neighboring areas, leading to economic development and cultural exchange. Today, the Koshigaya Ohashi serves as an everyday means of transportation for local residents and has also become a popular spot for its scenic beauty and tourism. From the bridge, one can enjoy views of the flowing Edo River and the surrounding landscape, making it a cherished symbol of the region. The Koshigaya Ohashi is an important part of Koshigaya City's history and development. It is a familiar presence for local residents and a source of pride for the community.
